Types Of Festivals

Dussehra

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a Hindu festival celebrated in India and other parts of the world. It marks the victory of good over evil and commemorates the triumph of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana.

Holi

Holi is a vibrant and joyous Hindu festival celebrated mainly in India and Nepal. The festival signifies the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil.

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most significant Hindu festivals celebrated worldwide. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Vinayaka Chavithi

Vinayaka Chaviti, also known as Ganesh Chaturthi, is a Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity. It is widely celebrated in India and other parts of the world.

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a major Hindu festival celebrated in India and other parts of the Indian subcontinent. It usually falls in the month of September or October, on the tenth day of the Hindu lunar calendar month called Ashwin. Holi

Holi is a vibrant and joyous festival celebrated primarily in India and Nepal, but it has also gained popularity in other parts of the world. It typically takes place in the spring, usually in March, and marks the arrival of springtime and the triumph of good over evil. One of the main highlights of Holi is the playful throwing of colorful powders and water at each other. Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most important Hindu festivals celebrated by millions of people worldwide. It signifies the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. The festival usually takes place in October or November and lasts for five days. Each day holds significance and is associated with different customs and rituals. During Diwali, people decorate their homes with colorful lights, diyas (oil lamps), and rangoli (decorative patterns made with colored powders or rice). It is believed that the lights and decorations invite the blessings of deities and bring prosperity and good fortune. Click below link to know more about Diwali. Diwali

Vinayaka Chavithi

Vinayaka Chavithi, also known as Ganesh Chaturthi, is a Hindu festival celebrated to honor Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god of wisdom and prosperity. The festival usually falls in the Hindu month of Bhadrapada, which is between August and September. During Vinayaka Chavithi, devotees bring idols or images of Lord Ganesha into their homes or public pandals. The idol is worshipped with devotion and offerings such as flowers, coconuts, sweets, and modak (a special sweet dumpling).
